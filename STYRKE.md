### Kravspesifikasjon bør inneholde:
- rules and regulations
- energy system
- kinematic (just considering position, velocity and acceleration without considering forces involved) and kinetic (movement which is effected by momentum) demands of the sport
- high-risk injury areas (rib, back, shoulder, wrist, elbow, knee)
- ROM - Range Of Movement (hvordan S&C kan støtte utvikling av spesifikke ROMs)
- MODEL A: prioritise physiological factors over the technical efficiency of the rowing style
  - maximal force expression (Fmax) rather than developing ROM and force capabilities 
  - emphasis on "strengt-endurance" performed for repetitions and tempos, replicating the number of strokes and stroke rate during a race (usually as pre-competition block; konkurranseforberedende periode, f.eks. inkludere hard sirkeltrening med 14 øvelser i takt 30-32 i programmet)

### Litt om å strekke skulder i catch
- Poor core stability: When the shoulders are overextended, it indicates that the core muscles are not engaged to provide stability. This means the lumbar spine takes on more of the force, especially as fatigue sets in.
- Lack of flexibility: A common reason for poor form is tightness in the hips and hamstrings. The body compensates for a lack of motion in the hips by creating excessive flexion at the spine, leading to more strain on the lower back.
- Increased spinal flexion: Shoulder overextension at the catch often goes hand-in-hand with rounding or flexing the lower back. The ideal form is a straight, neutral spine to transfer power efficiently.
